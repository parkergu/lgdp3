Abstract

Imitation learning offers an effective paradigm for teaching robots, yet acquiring robust policies for complex skills often requires numerous demonstrations. 3D Diffusion Policy (DP3), which operates on point cloud data, learns effective and generalizable skills with greater sample efficiency. A key limitation of DP3 is that it processes the entire visual observation equally, failing to distinguish task-relevant objects from irrelevant background, which ultimately limits its manipulation capabilities.

To address this limitation, we introduce 3D Visual Attention Diffusion Policy (3DVA), which leverages a visual attention module within its visual encoder to selectively focus on the most relevant regions of observations. The policy then generates actions conditioned on these 3D visual representations and the robot's proprioception, utilizing a diffusion-based backbone.

In our experiments across 8 simulation tasks, 3DVA surpasses the strong baseline (DP3) with a 12.4% relative improvement. Furthermore, on 3 real-world robot tasks, 3DVA outperforms DP3, achieving an impressive average 76% success rate with only 50 demonstrations. It also exhibits excellent generalization across variations in object appearance, lighting conditions, and object instances. Our extensive evaluations highlight the critical role of visual attention in learning robust 3D representations for robotic manipulation.

Overview

Overview of the proposed system. The proposed system comprises three key components: the data collection system, the 3D visuomotor policy learning method, and the real-world deployment. This integrated system enables the robot to execute autonomous skills in diverse real-world scenes.

Policy Overview

Overview of policy learning. The policy learning comprises two key components: Perception and Decision. (a) Perception: 3DVA uses single-view point clouds to observe its environment. These point clouds are converted into compact 3D representations by a lightweight Conv1D encoder and a visual attention module. (b) Decision: 3DVA generates actions conditioning on these 3D representations and the robot’s states, utilizing a diffusion-based backbone.

Real-world Experiments

To evaluate the performance of 3DVA in the real world, we conducted experiments on 3 tasks: Stack, Pour, and Hang.

Stack: The gripper grabs a block, and puts it on another block.

Pour: The gripper grabs the teapot handle, moves to the teacup, and pours the water.

Hang: The gripper grabs the mug handle, and hangs it on the mug rack.
